Subject: ROI Lens — Digital Audit & Strategic Budget Reallocation (₹100 Crore Portfolio)

CRITICAL RISK IDENTIFIED: Bot Traffic Wasting 48.3% of Spend
We audited last quarter's ₹101.8 Crore digital marketing expenditure. Our bot-filtering algorithm flagged **1,952 high-frequency user profiles** generating over **193,940 fraudulent click/impression events** (34.2% of all digital logs). This bot traffic generated **0 purchases** but drained **₹49.2 Crore** in pricing model fees. defunding these segments represents immediate financial salvage.

1. The Last-Click Attribution Fallacy

Our legacy last-click attribution model severely overstates the value of bottom-of-funnel conversion tools like Marketplace and Google Search. By ignoring user journeys, it hides that Instagram and YouTube act as vital top-of-funnel primers that drive the initial awareness needed for later searches and purchases.

2. Multi-Touch Probabilistic Findings

Our Markov Chain removal-effect framework and Shapley Value game-theory analysis show that Instagram's true contribution is **nearly double** what last-click indicates. For instance, in Brand B01, Instagram accounts for **33.5% of true attributed conversions**, yet last-click only credited it for actual final clicks. Instagram acts as a massive driver of downstream traffic.

RECOMMENDED STRATEGY: Constrained Portfolio Reallocation
For next quarter, we recommend allocating **₹10 Crore per brand** (₹100 Crore total) using our scipy-optimized budget framework. By shifting money toward high-performing priming channels and capping underperforming channels, we project a **portfolio-wide conversion uplift of 3.2%**, yielding hundreds of additional high-value consumer sales with no increase in spend.

Bot Traffic Mechanics Signature of a Fraud Pattern

Our analysis uncovered a highly structured bot network mimicking normal user journeys to inflate CTR metrics and CPM/CPC billings. The bot signature is characterized by:

  • Repetitive Activity: Bots generated exactly 60 to 140 events per user, whereas 99% of normal users had 1 to 10 events.
  • Zero Downstream Action: Bot journeys had exactly 50% impressions and 50% clicks. They logged 0 Add-to-Cart and 0 Purchase events.
  • Equally Distributed Fraud: Bot impressions and clicks were divided almost perfectly evenly across all five advertising channels.
Wasted Cost Calculation:
We mapped CPM and CPC rates directly to the volume of bot impressions and clicks. CPC campaigns (Google Search, Marketplace) suffered the worst financial losses because their Cost-Rate is paid per click, which bots triggered continuously.
